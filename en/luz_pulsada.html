What is Facial Pulsed Light?

Intense Pulsed Light (IPL) is an advanced technology used in aesthetic medicine to improve skin quality and treat various conditions. It is used to reduce sun spots, facial veins, rosacea, and signs of aging.

Unlike conventional lasers, which emit a single wavelength, IPL emits multiple wavelengths, making it more versatile and effective for treating different skin problems. It is a safe, non-invasive procedure with visible results from the first sessions.

IPL treatment is recommended to improve skin health and appearance, offering benefits such as:

  • üîπ Reducing redness and rosacea: Minimizes the appearance of dilated capillaries and redness.
  • üîπ Improving skin texture and firmness: Stimulates collagen production, achieving smoother and more luminous skin.

If you have sun spots, dull skin, or want to improve the uniformity of your face, IPL may be an ideal option for you.

Does it have risks or side effects?

IPL is a safe treatment when performed by professionals. However, some mild side effects may include:

  • üîπ Mild redness in the treated area.
  • üîπ Temporary swelling or sensation of heat on the skin.
  • üîπ Temporary darkening of some spots before elimination.

In exceptional cases, there may be changes in pigmentation. Therefore, it is essential to follow the professional's indications and avoid sun exposure after treatment.

How to prepare for the treatment?

To obtain the best results and minimize risks, it is recommended to:

  • üîπ Avoid sun exposure and the use of self-tanners at least two weeks before.
  • üîπ Do not apply creams with acids or retinol on the skin in the previous days.
  • üîπ Keep the skin well hydrated and free of irritations.

The number of sessions depends on each patient, but generally between 4 and 6 sessions are recommended, with an interval of 4 weeks. Results are progressive, reaching their best effect three months after treatment.
