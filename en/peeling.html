What is a Peeling?

Chemical peeling is an aesthetic procedure that uses chemical solutions to deeply exfoliate the skin. Its goal is to stimulate cell regeneration, removing damaged surface layers and revealing more luminous, uniform, and smooth skin. Depending on the type of acid and concentration used, the peeling can be superficial, medium, or deep.

What is it performed for?

Chemical peeling is used to visibly improve skin texture and quality. It is ideal for treating:

  • Wrinkles and expression lines: Attenuates fine lines and improves skin elasticity.
  • Spots and pigmentations: Reduces sun spots, hyperpigmentations, and melasma.
  • Acne and scars: Improves skin texture, smoothing acne marks and reducing breakouts.

Possible side effects

Although peeling is a safe treatment, temporary effects such as redness, sensitivity, flaking, and changes in pigmentation may occur. It is key to have it done by an experienced professional to minimize risks and obtain the best results.

Preparation before treatment

Before undergoing a peeling, it is recommended to avoid sun exposure and not use strong exfoliants for at least a week prior. It is also advisable to inform the professional about any product or medication you use in your routine to adapt the treatment according to your needs.

How is the procedure performed?

During the session, the professional cleanses the skin and applies the chemical solution, which acts for a certain time. It is normal to feel a slight sensation of heat or itching while the product acts. Then, the skin is neutralized and a soothing cream is applied. Depending on the type of peeling, recovery may take from a few hours to several days.

Expected results

The skin looks smoother, more uniform, and luminous after the peeling. To obtain optimal results and maintain them, it is key to follow a post-treatment care plan, which includes daily use of sunscreen.
