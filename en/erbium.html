What is the Erbium Laser?

The Erbium laser is an advanced technology used to improve skin quality by removing imperfections such as wrinkles, scars, and spots. This procedure works by removing superficial layers of the skin and stimulating collagen production, resulting in smoother, firmer, and rejuvenated skin. It is an ideal option for those looking to improve their appearance without resorting to invasive treatments.

What is it used for?

Erbium laser treatment is effective for:

  • Reducing fine wrinkles: Especially in delicate areas like around the eyes and mouth.
  • Improving skin texture: Unifies tone and leaves skin smoother and more luminous.
  • Removing sun spots and acne scars: Effectively acts on pigmentation and skin marks.

Risks and side effects

The Erbium laser is a safe treatment when performed by trained professionals. However, some temporary side effects may occur, such as:

  • Redness and sensitivity in the treated skin.
  • Mild swelling, which disappears in a few days.
  • Peeling in the first 48-72 hours as part of the regeneration process.

What to expect from the treatment?

Before starting the procedure, an anesthetic cream is applied to minimize any discomfort. During the session, slight heat sensation is felt on the skin, followed by redness and possible mild inflammation, which is normal.

Recovery varies according to the treatment intensity. In general, the skin may be red with mild peeling for 3 to 5 days. It is recommended to avoid sun exposure and use broad-spectrum sunscreen during this period.

How to prepare for the treatment

To guarantee the best results, it is recommended:

  • Avoid prolonged sun exposure before the procedure.
  • Do not use exfoliating or irritating products on the skin in the previous days.
  • Keep skin hydrated and free of makeup before the session.

During the procedure

During the session, the professional will apply the laser on the selected areas to remove superficial skin layers and stimulate cell regeneration. The procedure duration varies between 30 minutes and 1 hour, depending on the area to treat. A cooling system may be used to improve patient comfort.
