Overview

Thin lips can be an aesthetic concern for many people, especially as we age or lose volume in that area. This condition can make the mouth look less defined and affect facial harmony. At our Aesthetic Center, we offer various treatments that can help you recover volume and definition in your lips, restoring a youthful and balanced appearance.

Why do I have thin lips and how can they be improved?

  • Natural aging: As we age, we lose collagen and hyaluronic acid, which causes volume decrease in the lips. Treatments like hyaluronic acid fillers can restore lost volume, creating fuller and defined lips.
  • Genetics: Some people naturally have thinner lips. In these cases, hyaluronic acid fillers are an excellent option to increase volume naturally.
  • External factors: Prolonged sun exposure and smoking can accelerate volume loss in the lips. In these cases, treatments like chemical peel or Platelet Rich Plasma (PRP) help revitalize the skin and improve its quality.

What type of treatments exist to improve thin lips?

At our Aesthetic Center, we offer different options to improve the appearance of thin lips, naturally and with lasting results. Some of the most recommended treatments are:

  • Sculptra: Stimulates collagen production in lips for a gradual volume increase.
  • Platelet Rich Plasma (PRP): Improves lip skin quality, making them softer and firmer.
  • Accent Prime Radiofrequency: Non-invasive treatment that improves skin firmness around the lips, providing definition.
  • Hyaluronic Acid Fillers: Restore volume and shape to lips, giving them a younger and more natural look.

Do thin lips worsen over time?

Yes, with aging and other factors like sun exposure or smoking, lips tend to become thinner over time. If not treated, this volume loss can accentuate. Treatments like hyaluronic acid fillers or facial radiofrequency can prevent further deterioration and improve lip appearance.

What types of thin lips exist?

  • Thin lips by genetics: People who naturally have little volume in their lips.
  • Thin lips by aging: Volume loss in lips due to reduction of collagen and elastin with age.

What results can I expect with the treatments offered?

Treatment results vary according to the type of technique and individual needs of each patient. In general, you can expect fuller and well-defined lips. Treatments like hyaluronic acid fillers provide immediate results, while options like Sculptra or PRP offer gradual and long-lasting improvement.
