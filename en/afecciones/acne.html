Overview

Acne is a common skin condition that mainly affects adolescents, but can also persist into adulthood. It appears when pores become blocked with sebum and dead skin cells, resulting in blackheads, pimples, and inflamed nodules. At our Aesthetic Center, we offer personalized solutions to treat acne and improve the overall appearance of the skin.

Why do I have acne and how can it be eliminated?

  • Hormonal changes: Increased sebum production is usually caused by hormonal fluctuations, especially during adolescence. Treatments like chemical peeling or Q-Switch laser can help reduce oil and improve skin appearance.
  • Dead cell accumulation: When dead cells clog pores, acne tends to appear. A good treatment with platelet-rich plasma (PRP) or mesotherapy can rejuvenate the skin and minimize breakouts.
  • Bacteria and external factors: The presence of bacteria and factors like pollution also contribute to acne. In these cases, intense pulsed light (IPL) and Accent Prime radiofrequency can be effective options to improve texture and control the problem.

What type of treatments exist to improve acne?

At our Aesthetic Center, we have a variety of advanced treatments that adapt to the individual needs of each patient with acne. Some of the most notable treatments include:

  • Chemical peeling: Removes superficial skin layers to reduce breakouts and improve overall texture.
  • Q-Switch Laser: Ideal for reducing acne scars and hyperpigmentation caused by previous breakouts.
  • Platelet Rich Plasma (PRP): Rejuvenates the skin and accelerates cell regeneration, improving acne scars and spots.
  • Facial radiofrequency with Accent Prime: Helps improve the appearance of acne-affected skin by stimulating collagen.

Does acne get worse over time?

Acne can worsen if not treated properly. In the long term, scars, spots, and recurrent breakouts can affect both the skin and self-esteem. With treatments like skinboosters and Profhilo, you can improve skin quality and prevent long-term damage.

What types of acne are there?

  • Inflammatory acne: Characterized by pustules and nodules that cause inflammation and redness on the skin.
  • Comedonal acne: Appears as blackheads and pimples due to pore clogging by excess sebum and dead cells.

What results can I expect with the treatments you offer?

Results depend on the type of acne and the chosen treatment. In general, you can expect a significant improvement in skin texture, fewer breakouts, and a reduction in acne scars or spots. Treatments like Q-Switch laser and mesotherapy can generate progressive results, while options like hyaluronic acid fillers offer immediate improvements in skin appearance.
