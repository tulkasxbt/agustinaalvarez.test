Overview

Dark circles are one of the most frequent consultations at our Aesthetic Center. They appear as darkening or hollows under the eyes and can have multiple causes, such as genetics, aging or lack of sleep. If you want to improve the appearance of your dark circles, we have a wide variety of treatments that will help you rejuvenate your look effectively.

Why do I have dark circles and how can they be eliminated?

  • Genetic factors: Hereditary dark circles are common and usually more difficult to eliminate with traditional creams. Treatments like hyaluronic acid filler can be ideal for improving lost volume in the area.
  • Lack of sleep: Tiredness can also aggravate dark circles. At our Aesthetic Center, we recommend combining a healthy routine with laser treatments to lighten skin tone.
  • Aging: As we get older, skin loses collagen and elasticity. Treatments like facial radiofrequency can help tighten and improve skin texture under the eyes.

What type of treatments exist to improve dark circles?

There are several treatments available at our Aesthetic Center to improve dark circles. Some of the most effective are:

  • Hyaluronic Acid Filler: Ideal for people with deep or sunken dark circles.
  • Q-Switch Laser: Perfect for lightening dark circles caused by hyperpigmentation.
  • Chemical Peel: Used to improve skin texture and tone.
  • Facial Radiofrequency: Recommended to tighten skin under the eyes and improve its firmness.
  • Erbium Laser: Helps rejuvenate the skin, improving dark circles caused by aging and loss of elasticity.

Do dark circles worsen over time?

Dark circles can worsen if not treated properly. Lack of sleep and stress continue to contribute to their appearance, while aging causes greater loss of elasticity and collagen. Therefore, it is important to address dark circles early and consider treatments like hyaluronic acid filler or radiofrequency treatment to prevent their worsening.

What types of dark circles are there?

  • Vascular dark circles: Caused by circulation problems, they usually have a blue or violet tone.
  • Pigmented dark circles: Related to hyperpigmentation, they have a dark brown tone.
  • Structural dark circles: Generated by hollows in the area, they look deeper and sunken.

What results can I expect with the treatments offered?

Results of dark circles treatments vary depending on the method used. Hyaluronic acid fillers offer an immediate improvement in area volume, while Q-Switch laser treatments may require several sessions to lighten the dark area. At our Aesthetic Center, we design a personalized plan for each patient, ensuring results that rejuvenate your look and return freshness to your face.
