Overview

Localized adiposity is a type of fat that accumulates in specific areas of the body, such as the abdomen, flanks, thighs, or buttocks, and can be difficult to eliminate even with diet and exercise. At our Aesthetic Center, we offer non-invasive treatments that help reduce this localized fat effectively and without surgery, restoring a more harmonious contour to your body.

Why do I have localized adiposity and how can it be eliminated?

  • Genetics: Some people are predisposed to accumulate fat in specific areas of the body, making localized adiposity difficult to eliminate with traditional methods.
  • Lifestyle: Lack of exercise and a high-calorie diet can aggravate fat accumulation in certain areas.
  • Hormonal changes: Factors such as pregnancy or menopause can alter fat distribution in the body.

    • To eliminate localized adiposity, at our Aesthetic Center, we offer treatments such as Accent Prime radiofrequency and mesotherapy, which act on fat deposits safely and effectively.

What type of treatments exist to improve localized adiposity?

At our Aesthetic Center, we have a variety of non-invasive treatments to treat localized adiposity without surgery. Some of the most effective are:

  • Accent Prime Radiofrequency: This treatment uses radiofrequency energy to reduce localized fat, while also tightening the skin in the process.
  • Mesotherapy: Injects substances that help dissolve fat in specific areas, facilitating its natural elimination by the body.
  • PB Serum Enzymes: This technique breaks down fat cells locally, helping to reduce adiposity in complicated areas.

Does localized adiposity get worse over time?

If left untreated, localized adiposity can worsen over time, especially if combined with factors such as aging and a sedentary lifestyle. That's why it is important to act as soon as possible to prevent its progression. Treatments like mesotherapy or Accent Prime radiofrequency can prevent fat accumulation from getting worse.

What types of localized adiposity are there?

  • Subcutaneous adiposity: It is the fat that accumulates just under the skin and, although difficult to eliminate, responds well to non-invasive treatments.
  • Visceral adiposity: This type of fat is found around internal organs and is not treated with aesthetic procedures, but with lifestyle changes.

What results can I expect with the treatments you offer?

The results of treatments for localized adiposity vary depending on the treated area and the method used. In general, you can expect a significant reduction of fat in problem areas such as the abdomen, thighs, or buttocks. Treatments like Accent Prime radiofrequency and PB Serum enzymes are known for offering visible results from the first sessions.
