Overview

Rosacea is a chronic skin condition that mainly affects the face, causing redness, inflammation and the appearance of visible blood vessels. Although the exact cause of rosacea is not yet completely understood, it is known that genetic and environmental factors play an important role. At our Aesthetic Center, we offer a wide variety of treatments to control rosacea symptoms and significantly improve skin appearance.

Why do I have rosacea and how can it be eliminated?

  • Genetic factors: Many people with rosacea have a family history of the disease. It is not possible to eliminate rosacea, but treatments like Accent Prime radiofrequency and chemical peels can help control outbreaks.
  • Environmental factors: Sun exposure, stress and certain foods or drinks can trigger or worsen symptoms. Treatment with Q-Switch Laser and intense pulsed light is ideal for reducing inflammation and redness.

What type of treatments exist to improve rosacea?

At our Aesthetic Center, we offer several effective treatments for rosacea, designed to reduce inflammation, redness and improve skin texture:

  • Intense Pulsed Light (IPL): Ideal for treating redness and visible blood vessels caused by rosacea.
  • Q-Switch Laser: Helps reduce pigmentation and improve overall skin appearance.

Does rosacea worsen over time?

Yes, rosacea can worsen if not treated properly. Over time, redness can become more persistent, and blood vessels can become more visible. To prevent its progression, it is essential to control outbreaks with treatments like intense pulsed light and platelet rich plasma (PRP), which can help calm the skin and prevent future episodes.

What types of rosacea are there?

  • Erythematotelangiectatic rosacea: Characterized by persistent redness and visible blood vessels.
  • Papulopustular rosacea: Accompanied by breakouts similar to acne, in addition to redness.

What results can I expect with the treatments offered?

Rosacea treatments can significantly improve skin appearance, reducing redness and calming inflammation. Treatments like intense pulsed light and chemical peel offer visible results in a few sessions and Q-Switch Laser provide long-term improvements.
